Works & Writings

Literary Masterpieces That Sparked a Revolution

Major Literary Works

Rizal's writings were powerful weapons against colonial oppression. Through his novels, poems, and letters, he exposed injustices and inspired Filipinos to fight for their rights and dignity.

1887

Noli Me Tángere

The Social Cancer

Rizal’s first novel which exposed social injustices, hypocrisy, and abuses by Spanish friars and officials. It follows Crisostomo Ibarra and María Clara and revealed the “social cancer” in colonial society.

Impact

Awakened Filipino nationalism and ignited the desire for peaceful reforms through education and awareness.

1891

El Filibusterismo

The Reign of Greed

A darker sequel to Noli, following Simoun (Ibarra in disguise) who plots revenge. It examines the consequences of revenge and growing revolution.

Impact

Inspired revolutionary sentiments and foreshadowed the coming Philippine Revolution.

Unfinished

Makamisa

After the Mass

An unfinished novel written in Tagalog that satirizes town life and the clergy, using humor and lighter criticism to show the people's ignorance and gossip.

Impact

Shows Rizal’s link to the common Filipino and his intention to use the native language for social awareness.

Poems & Letters

Poetic Farewells, Youthful Calls, and Courageous Letters

This collection highlights Rizal’s poems — from the moving Mi Último Adiós to youthful pieces that urged love of country and language — alongside letters that defended education, women’s rights, and national dignity.

Summary

Poems show Rizal’s deep love and reflective side; the letters reveal his earnest advocacy for education, women's progress, and moral courage.
