The Life of Dr. Jose Rizal

A Journey Through Time: 1861 - 1896

Chronological Timeline

Follow the remarkable journey of José Rizal from his birth to his martyrdom

1861

Birth and Early Childhood

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna. He was the seventh child of Francisco Mercado Rizal and Teodora Alonso Realonda.

1872-1877

Education in Manila

Rizal studied at Ateneo Municipal de Manila, where he excelled in academics and won numerous awards. He later enrolled at the University of Santo Tomas to study medicine.

1882-1887

Studies in Europe

Rizal traveled to Spain to continue his medical studies at Universidad Central de Madrid. He also studied in Paris and Heidelberg, becoming a skilled ophthalmologist while writing his first novel.

1887

Noli Me Tangere Published

His groundbreaking novel "Noli Me Tangere" (Touch Me Not) was published in Berlin, exposing the injustices of Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines.

1891

El Filibusterismo Published

Rizal published his second novel "El Filibusterismo" (The Reign of Greed) in Ghent, Belgium, which served as a sequel to Noli Me Tangere and depicted a darker vision of Philippine society.

1892

Return to Philippines & La Liga Filipina

Rizal returned to the Philippines and founded La Liga Filipina, a civic organization aimed at social reforms. He was arrested and exiled to Dapitan shortly after.

1892-1896

Exile in Dapitan

During his exile in Dapitan, Rizal practiced medicine, taught students, engaged in farming and business, and continued his scientific research and artistic pursuits.

1896

Martyrdom

On December 30, 1896, José Rizal was executed by firing squad at Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park) in Manila. His death sparked the Philippine Revolution and he became a symbol of Filipino nationalism.

Family, Travels & Achievements

Discover the personal side of our national hero

The Rizal Family

Born into a prosperous family of farmers and merchants, Rizal was raised with strong values of education, faith, and love for country.

Travels Across Europe

Rizal traveled extensively across Europe, visiting Spain, France, Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and Italy, absorbing knowledge and culture.

A Man of Many Talents

Rizal was a polymath: doctor, writer, artist, sculptor, educator, farmer, historian, and linguist who spoke over 20 languages.
